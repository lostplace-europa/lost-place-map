Checklist entries (with text and status):

Flashlight: A good flashlight is your constant companion on every Lost Places tour. It ensures that you can keep an eye on everything even in the darkest corners, recognize hidden dangers in good time and never miss a detail. It can also help you discover clues about the history of the place. Helpful: Yes

First aid kit: A first aid kit is your safety net. It helps you to treat minor injuries immediately so that you can continue your tour safely. From plasters to bandages - everything you need in case something happens. Helpful: Yes

Gloves: Robust gloves reliably protect your hands from sharp edges, debris and rusty nails. They give you the confidence to explore even difficult corners without injuring yourself. Helpful: Yes

Respirator mask: A breathing mask protects you from dust, mold spores and other harmful substances that lurk in old buildings in particular. So you can breathe easy and enjoy your tour. Helpful: Optional

Protective goggles: Safety goggles protect your eyes from dust, splinters or falling particles. They are indispensable, especially when entering buildings with loose plaster or glass debris. Helpful: Optional

Helmet: A helmet protects you from falling parts and gives you safety in buildings with unstable ceilings or attics. This allows you to concentrate fully on your discovery tour. Helpful: Optional

Climbing rope/safety harness: A climbing rope or safety harness gives you additional safety if you want to explore higher or hard-to-reach areas. This means you are ideally equipped for challenging lost places. Helpful: Optional

Hand sanitizer: Lost places are often full of dust and dirt. With hand sanitizer, you can quickly ensure hygiene after an exploration. Helpful: Optional

Walkie-talkie/radio: A walkie-talkie or radio is worth its weight in gold, especially when there is no cell phone network. This allows you to stay in contact with your team and call for help in an emergency. Helpful: Optional

Emergency whistle: An emergency whistle helps you to draw attention to yourself in dangerous situations. It can save lives, especially in remote buildings. Helpful: Optional

Spare batteries/power bank: Spare batteries or a power bank are essential to ensure that your technology doesn't run out of power. This means you are always supplied with power, even on longer tours. Helpful: Optional

Multitool or knife: A multitool or knife is a real all-rounder on Lost Places tours. Whether for small repairs, cutting or emergencies - you are well equipped. Helpful: Optional
